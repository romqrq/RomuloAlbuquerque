About Me

Pleased to have you seeing my CV! My name is Romulo Albuquerque, Software Development student at Code Institute in Dublin - Ireland.

I am very competitive (especially to myself) but I'm also ethical and don't think I need to step over other people to reach my goals. I have a high drive and energy to back it up.

I enjoy leading through trust, creating the conditions for trust to flourish and helping others. I am always looking to improve myself and being better than yesterday.

Interests

Music

I absolutely love music. I'm a guitarrist, I can also play base guitar and sing. I've also been learning drums, piano/keyboard and harmonica. Still have the violin on the queue.

Sports

I played semi-pro American Football as a slot receiver and running back I'm also a red belt in karate. I used to compete in rowing and although I don't watch many foorball games I do enjoy playing.

Last but not least one of my biggest passions is race driving although my experience hasn't passed the go-karting stage I can't explain how good I feel racing.

Learning

I'm very curious. I want to know more. From fungi that takes over insects nervous systems to wondering if our big bang means our universe came from the other side of a black hole and not necessarily a massive explosion.

Fixing Things

I do enjoy fixing things. As a child I did break apart most of my toys (and some innocent home appliances) which had some spare parts when I tried to put them back together. Since I can remember I helped my father fixing the car, plastering and painting the walls in the house and things like that. We were the real life "Bob the builder". As a result I've been learning more at every fixing attempt as it involves research and gauging my own abilities against the challenge.

Design and Solutions Development

I'm always looking around for alternatives to our problems. I have been developing solutions that combine existing technologies in a unique way that prioritizes the environment and practicity. They go from small city vehicles with electric/human propulsion to aquatic public transport with adjustable draft to adapt to shallow sections of rivers and going under bridges.

Since I acquired an arduino board, I've been developing ideas for home automation solutions and some other fun but less useful gadgets such as a light theremin for example.
